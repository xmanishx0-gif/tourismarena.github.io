India Gate

India Gate, located in the heart of New Delhi, is one of the most iconic war memorials in India. Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, the monument was completed in 1931 as a tribute to the 70,000 Indian soldiers who lost their lives during World War I and in various other battles. Standing at a height of about 42 meters, India Gate resembles the Arc de Triomphe in Paris in its architectural style, with a grand arch built using sandstone. The names of thousands of soldiers are inscribed on its walls, making it a powerful reminder of courage, sacrifice, and patriotism.

The monument is surrounded by wide roads, beautiful lawns, and a large open area which makes it a popular gathering place for both tourists and locals. At night, the India Gate is beautifully lit, creating a breathtaking sight that attracts visitors in large numbers. For decades, the area around India Gate has served as a space for family outings, picnics, and cultural events. The lush green gardens, vendors, and fountains give the place a lively and welcoming atmosphere, especially during evenings and weekends.
